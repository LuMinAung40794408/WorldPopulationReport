# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge
We pledge to create a respectful, inclusive, and collaborative environment for everyone contributing to the **World Population Report** project.  
All participants, regardless of role, are expected to contribute positively to teamwork and communication.

---

## Purpose
- Define how the team will operate and communicate effectively.
- Ensure accountability, fairness, and respect among members.
- Promote high standards of software quality and academic integrity.
- Support an inclusive, professional environment where all contributions are valued.

## Our Standards
Examples of positive behavior include:
- Using respectful and inclusive language
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the team
- Showing empathy toward other contributors

Unacceptable behavior includes:
- Harassment or discrimination of any kind
- Public or private insults
- Trolling, personal attacks, or political/religious arguments
- Any behavior that disrupts project collaboration

---

## Our Responsibilities
Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

**Communication**
- Maintain professional and respectful communication at all times.
- Respond to messages, emails, and requests within a reasonable timeframe (24 hours during weekdays).
- Use constructive language when giving feedback.

**Collaboration**
- Commit to assigned tasks and ensure quality of work.
- Share progress updates regularly during team meetings or via our project management tool.
- Merge code responsibly and avoid conflicts by pulling the latest changes before pushing new code.

**Accountability**
- Take ownership of assigned tasks and deadlines.
- Communicate challenges early and seek help when needed.
- Review teammatesâ€™ contributions thoughtfully during code reviews or report evaluation.

**Conflict Resolution**
- Address disagreements calmly and professionally.
- Seek consensus through discussion or involve the team lead if needed.
- Focus on solutions rather than assigning blame.
